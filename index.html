♦Paper Chameleon♦

  • Chameleons have a 360 degree view around them.
  • They like to live in warm tropical regions.
  • Chameleons live up to 2-10 years.

♦Paper Camel♦

  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  • There are over 160 words for camel in Arabic alone.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

♦Paper Cicada♦

  • There are around 3000 species of Cicadas.
  • Cicadas are herbivores.
  • They make a buzzing sound as a mating call.

♦Paper Panda♦

  • Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.
  • They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food.
  • Like domestic cats, giant pandas have vertical slits for pupils.

♦Paper Teddy♦

  • There are 8 different species of bear.
  • Bears have a biting force of over 1200 psi.
  • They can walk on their hind legs.

♦Paper Pigeon♦

  • Pigeons and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years. The first recordings of this date back to Mesopotamis, modern Iraq, in 3000bc.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.
